Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is the largest religious monument in the world, and the most famous of all the temples in Angkor. It was built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II as his state temple and capital city. The temple is a symbol of Cambodia, appearing on its national flag, and it is the country's prime attraction for visitors.

Bayon Temple

Bayon Temple is one of the most iconic and enigmatic temples in the Angkor Archaeological Park, located in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It was built in the late 12th or early 13th century as the state temple of King Jayavarman VII, a prominent Khmer ruler. The temple is renowned for its unique architectural style and intricate carvings.

Ta Prohm

Ta Prohm is a captivating temple located in the Angkor Archaeological Park in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It was built in the late 12th and early 13th centuries during the reign of King Jayavarman VII as a Buddhist monastery and university. What sets Ta Prohm apart from other temples in the area is its unique blend of ancient architecture and the encroaching jungle, creating a mystical and atmospheric experience for visitors.

Preah Khan

Preah Khan is a large and impressive temple complex located in the Angkor Archaeological Park in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It was built in the 12th century during the reign of King Jayavarman VII as a Buddhist monastery and university. The temple is known for its intricate architecture, extensive carvings, and historical significance.

Phnom Bakheng

Phnom Bakheng is a Hindu and Buddhist temple located in the Angkor Archaeological Park in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It is one of the oldest temples in the area, built in the late 9th century during the reign of King Yasovarman I. The temple is situated on a hilltop, providing panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including the famous Angkor Wat temple.

Culture

Angkor Wat is not only a magnificent architectural marvel but also a symbol of Cambodia's rich cultural heritage. The temple complex reflects the artistic and religious achievements of the Khmer Empire, showcasing intricate carvings, bas-reliefs, and sculptures that depict Hindu mythology, historical events, and everyday life during that era.
