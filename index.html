Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is the largest religious monument in the world, and the most famous of all the temples in Angkor. It was built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II as his state temple and capital city.

Bayon Temple

Bayon Temple is one of the most iconic and enigmatic temples in the Angkor Archaeological Park, located in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It was built in the late 12th or early 13th century as the state temple of King Jayavarman VII, a prominent Khmer ruler.

Ta Prohm

Ta Prohm is a captivating temple located in the Angkor Archaeological Park in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It was built in the late 12th and early 13th centuries during the reign of King Jayavarman VII as a Buddhist monastery and university.

Preah Khan

Preah Khan is a large and impressive temple complex located in the Angkor Archaeological Park in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It was built in the 12th century during the reign of King Jayavarman VII as a Buddhist monastery and university.

Phnom Bakheng

Phnom Bakheng is a Hindu and Buddhist temple located in the Angkor Archaeological Park in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It is one of the oldest temples in the area, built in the late 9th century during the reign of King Yasovarman I.
